Research Interests

My initial background focused on applications for wireless communications and Positioning, Navigation, and Timing (PNT) technologies. Moving forward, I am interested in pivoting out into broader applications of signal and information processing and more emphasis on theory. At Rice, I will be rotating in the labs of Professors Veeraraghavan, Knightly, and Segarra. I look forward to updating this website as my research interests continue to evolve.

Selected Undergraduate Projects

LEO-based Doppler Navigation

Summer 2026

Research internship at the Johns Hopkins Applied Physics Laboratory. Analyzing and simulating the reliability of LEO Doppler navigation in adversarial conditions, emphasizing the integrated Doppler technique and error-state Kalman filtering.

GPS Spoofing Characterization

2024-2025

Year-long self-directed research project. Developed a hybrid unsupervised anomaly detection model using an LSTM autoencoder and isolation forest to characterize GPS spoofing attacks.

Superconvergence of Finite Differences

Summer 2025

CSU Fresno STEAM Pathways Program. Coinvestigated the role of centeredness in superconvergence of finite difference approximations through linear algebra proofs and numerical experiments.

Blind Spatial Awareness Helmet

2025

Senior design project. Codeveloped a wearable perception helmet using Intel RealSense and Jetson Nano to improve spatial awareness for blind and visually impaired users.
